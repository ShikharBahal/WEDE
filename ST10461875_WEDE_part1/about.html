Our History

WorldWide Animal Protection was founded in 2007 by Adel Malan, who recognized the need for a wildlife trauma and rehabilitation center. Developer Rob Taylor sponsored the building of the WorldWide Animal Protection clinic, which opened its doors on the 1st of January 2008.

Our Work

WorldWide Animal Protection (WWAP) is a leading wildlife rehabilitation center, admitting over 2000 patients annually. Known for its strict "hands-off" approach, WWAP ensures animals are neither tamed nor habituated, promoting natural wild behaviors. This policy is crucial for successful rehabilitation and reintegration.

WWAP has aided the recovery and release of 27 Endangered, Threatened, or Protected species. In 2019, they executed a major rescue operation, saving over 2000 Amur Falcons.

Collaborating with organizations like Wild Tomorrow and local Nature Reserves, WWAP focuses on conservation and species reintroduction. Their research initiatives, such as The Tortoise Project with A Rocha and Ezemvelo Wildlife, aim to reform laws and protocols for wildlife rehabilitation.

WorldWide Animal Protection is collaborating with organizations such as Wild Tomorrow, whose primary goal is to purchase land for the conservation of animals, and locally declared Nature Reserves and Conservancies on the reintroduction of various species.
